The Founding Of Wending Flats

Wending Flats wasn’t so much founded by Connor Graves as it found him. Graves was a quiet man born in San Francisco in 1904, but when his train broke down on a journey from San Francisco to Salt Lake City, he fell in love with the still beauty of the desert. In 1932 he decided to leave everything behind to build a little cafe – the Coyote Kettle – in the middle of the desert, close enough to the railroad that customers would be carted right to him on the trains passing through. The Coyote Kettle served droves upon droves of hungry travelers, and as time went on a few of them just didn’t leave - and thus the town of Wending Flats was born.
